Research Statement (RS)

Date: 26 June 2023 | Author: Yong

My research focuses on the genetics and genomics in plants science to contribute to the food security, e.g., rice, maize, wheat, sorghum, soybean, and duckweed. I primarily work on genome assembly, genetic diversity, and whole genome variation using next-generation and long-read sequencing technologies. I am interested in how the variations affect biodiversity and agriculture traits, and also their independent evolution and coevolution (convergent) process under climate change and human selection. My research strategies range from the field (sample collection and phenotyping), lab (DNA, RNA, libraries and molecular genetics), computer (coding skills, computational biology and bioinformatics, e.g., analysis of large sequence datasets), and artificial intelligence (AI methods, e.g., machine learning). My future work will continue with what are doing now, and will also compile more AI strategies with applied research.

Key words: genetics, genomics, plant science, PacBio, Next generation sequence, high-performance computing, machine learning

Teaching Philosophy

Date: 24 August 2021-12 | Author: Yong

My goal is to be a good and a successful teacher, who hope students could have significant understanding of molecular biology after the course finished. My basic teaching philosophy is delivery, thinking, and inspiring. I hope them to gain the deep thinking about biology questions and try to find answers, and importantly, I would like to inspire them to think what have not be covered.
